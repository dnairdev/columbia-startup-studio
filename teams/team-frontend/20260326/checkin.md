# Look Back: Peer Review

**What worked when people used your product?**
The onboarding process was smooth, and users were excited to set goals for themselves during setup. The calendar feature was well-received, and the coaching page felt intuitive. Users had no trouble adding and editing workouts.

**What confused them or broke?**
During onboarding, some users didn't have certain lifts in their program, hadn't performed them before, or couldn't remember their maxes. Max inputs are now optional and supported by a dropdown menu with the ability to add and remove lifts, which has smoothed out this friction. The code system for adding a coach after initial sign-up was also broken, and has since been fixed.

**What's the biggest gap?**
The biggest gap was onboarding athletes who create accounts without a coach. The coach code flow has now been fixed, making it possible to add a coach at any point after sign-up. With workout sharing unblocked, the focus shifts to building a great experience for athletes training independently.

**Improvements shipped:**
- Max lift inputs are now optional, with a dropdown menu that supports adding and removing lifts
- Strength goals can be quantitative (max lifts) or qualitative, allowing athletes to write out personal goals
- Onboarding is faster for athletes with a coach, who can now skip questions their coach has already defined
- The exercise library has been expanded, with the ability to add custom exercises to the dropdown
- The coach code flow has been fixed so athletes can add a coach even after initial sign-up
- A post-workout debrief has been added, where athletes can log RPE and notes for future reference
- Calendar workouts are now color-graded by RPE, making it easy to spot training load at a glance
- Workout descriptions have been added to explain the purpose of each session within the broader program
- A "+" button on calendar days lets athletes add workouts, copy previous sessions, generate from their program, or create a custom one
- An auto-generate feature creates a weekly training plan based on a target timeframe or strength goal, taking into account injuries, goals, and current maxes
- A social feature has been added, allowing athletes to connect with friends, share workouts and progress, and celebrate strength accomplishments, similar to Strava but built for lifting

# Look Forward: Your Roadmap

**What do you still need to learn about your product?**
We need to understand how to balance serving team-based athletes and individual lifters who train independently. Now that the core functionality is more complete, we can better evaluate which audience finds the most value and where onboarding still creates friction. The "aha moment" will hit when coaches see their athletes actively following the programs they've sent out, and when independent athletes discover how easy it is to auto-generate a plan and track progress over time.

**What do you need to build between now and demo day?**
We need to continue refining overall app stability and polish the user experience across all the newly shipped features. The social layer needs further development to feel engaging and sticky. We also want to ensure the AI-generated workout plans are producing high-quality, personalized programs that athletes trust and stick with.

**What is your team's biggest open question?**
How do we build a product that works equally well for team athletes following a coach's program and for independent lifters who train with friends or follow their own programming?

**What does success look like on May 12?**
Success is a fully functional app actively used by my rowing team, with a social feature that creates genuine engagement and a strong foundation for supporting lifters who are not on teams.
