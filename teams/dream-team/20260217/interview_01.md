# Interview 01: Tobias Esche

**Role/Title:** Assistant Coach
**Organization:** Columbia University Men's Soccer (NCAA Division I)

---

## 1) Context

**Q: What's your role/title, sport, and level?**
A: D1 assistant soccer coach at Columbia University.

**Q: Roughly how many prospects do you seriously evaluate in a typical month?**
A: We receive about 250 recruiting emails per day. We usually watch 1–2 players fully per day. If we fully evaluate a player, the staff may watch 5–6 full games.

## 2) Current Workflow

**Q: How are players first discovered?**
A: Mostly through email or by seeing them play at events/showcases. Many players get their first look at events and then we reach out via email or text.

**Q: What happens if they are too young?**
A: If they are not at a recruitable age, we add them to our database to track.

**Q: What is your "first 30 seconds" evaluation process?**
A: In the first 30 seconds we look at how the player moves and their basic touch. If that checks the box we keep watching. If not, we move on.

## 3) Tools Used + What's Missing

**Q: What tools do you rely on today?**
A: Our main tool is Teamworks (ARMS). It holds data of all players and organizes notes with recruiting boards for each class. We also use YouTube, Hudl, and NCSA to watch players.

**Q: What do you like about those tools?**
A: They centralize player information and allow filtering by position, class, and star rating.

**Q: What's frustrating or incomplete?**
A: The system is only as good as the data you put in and it can be tedious. Sometimes we also use spreadsheets and that makes the work process more complex.

**Q: What do you wish the tools did that they don't?**
A: I would like to assign tasks to other coaches in Teamworks and set reminders to follow up.

## 4) Tracking + Organization

**Q: How do you keep prospects organized? Where do notes live?**
A: All notes live in ARMS (Teamworks). Sometimes it is hard for everyone to keep their notes up to date.

**Q: Where do things break down when juggling prospects?**
A: Follow-ups can fall through if reminders are not managed.

**Q: If you needed the "top 20 prospects at a position" tomorrow, how long would it take?**
A: Less than 1 minute. Players have star ratings and we can filter by class, position, and rating.
