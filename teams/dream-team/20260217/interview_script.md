# Recruiting Platform Interview Guide (Short)

## Goal

Understand current recruiting workflows and validate whether a **single app** that combines **TikTok-style highlight discovery** + **recruiting organization tools** solves real problems for **coaches** and **college-age athletes (18–22)**.

---

## Coach Interview (25–35 min)

### 1) Context (3 min)

- What's your role/title, sport, and level (D2/D3/NAIA/JUCO, club)?
- Roughly how many prospects do you seriously evaluate in a typical month?

### 2) Current workflow (8–10 min)

- Think about the most recent athlete you recruited — how did you first discover them?
- Where do most prospects come from (events, referrals, platforms, email/DMs)?
- What's your "first 30 seconds" evaluation process when you see film?

### 3) Tools used + what's missing (8–10 min)

- What tools do you rely on today for recruiting? (Hudl, YouTube, NCSA, Teamworks, spreadsheets, email, notes apps, etc.)
- What do you like about those tools — and what's frustrating or incomplete?
- What do you *wish* those tools did that they don't (search, context, organization, messaging, comparisons, workflow)?
- Where do things break down when you're juggling lots of prospects?

### 4) Tracking + organization (5–7 min)

- How do you keep prospects organized (stages, lists, boards)? Where do notes live?
- If you needed "top 20 prospects for X position" tomorrow, how long would it take — and why?

### 5) Communication + trust (4–6 min)

- How do athletes typically contact you? What feels helpful vs spammy?
- What safety/trust checks matter most (verified coaches, fake profiles, compliance constraints)?

### 6) Concept test (5–7 min)

**Paraphrased prompt (use verbatim):**

- Would you want a *single app* that gives you a TikTok-style reel of highlights **and** an organizational tool to manage recruiting — shortlists, notes, stages, and school/team context — in one place?

Follow-ups:

- What would make that genuinely useful for you weekly?
- What would you expect it to do better than your current tools?
- What would be a dealbreaker or something you'd never use?

### Close

- Can we follow up to test a prototype with you?
- Who else should we talk to (assistants/coordinators)?

---

## Athlete Interview (25–35 min)

### 1) Context + goals (3–4 min)

- What's your sport/position and current situation (JUCO/4-year/transfer exploring)?
- What are you trying to achieve in the next 3 months?

### 2) Recruiting struggles (8–10 min)

- What's the hardest part about getting recruited right now?
- Where do you get stuck most: getting seen, getting responses, knowing what to send, staying organized, or something else?
- Tell me about a time you felt momentum — and a time you felt ignored. What was different?

### 3) How they organize coaches (5–7 min)

- How do you keep track of all the coaches/programs you're talking to?
- What tools do you use (notes, spreadsheets, email folders, DMs)? What's messy or stressful about it?
- What do you wish you had to stay organized (reminders, statuses, templates, one inbox, etc.)?

### 4) Media + profile tools (6–8 min)

- What tools do you use to create and post your film/highlights (Hudl, YouTube, Instagram/TikTok, CapCut, iMovie, etc.)?
- Where do you keep your "main recruiting profile" today — and what don't you like about it?
- What stops you from posting more or updating your profile more often?

### 5) Concept test (5–7 min)

**Paraphrased prompt (use verbatim):**

- If there was one app where you could post highlights like a short-video feed **and** manage recruiting — coach messages, shortlists, and your profile — in one place, would that make recruiting easier for you? Why or why not?

Follow-ups:

- What would make you trust it and use it consistently?
- What would you need on day one for it to feel "worth it"?

### Close

- Can we follow up to test a prototype?
- Who else should we talk to (teammates, transfers, trainers)?

---

## What to Capture (quick notes)

- Current tools (exact names), biggest pain points (verbatim quotes)
- Workarounds they use today
- "Must-have" features they describe without prompting
- Reactions to the "single app" concept + dealbreakers
- Willingness to pilot + referrals to other participants
