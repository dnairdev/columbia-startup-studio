# Interview 02: Dean Kelliher

**Role/Title:** Goalkeeper
**Organization:** University of Denver Men's Soccer (NCAA Division I)

---

## 1) Context

**Q: What's your sport/position and current situation?**
A: I'm a goalkeeper at the University of Denver. I committed from club soccer and later experienced the transfer portal process, which is now a major part of college recruiting.

**Q: What are your goals during recruiting/transfer?**
A: Playing time and development. For goalkeepers especially, if you're behind an older starter you often need to transfer to actually play.

## 2) Recruiting Struggles

**Q: What was the hardest part about recruiting?**
A: Getting coaches to respond and knowing if they even watched your film.

**Q: How important is the transfer portal?**
A: Extremely important. A lot of recruiting now happens through transfers. Coaches actively look for players who already have college experience because it's less risky.

**Q: What problems did you face during the transfer process?**
A: Organization. Suddenly you're talking to many schools at once. You have to send film, transcripts, schedules, and answer calls quickly.

## 3) Organization

**Q: How did you keep track of coaches/programs?**
A: I used a spreadsheet with coach names, division, last contact, and interest level.

**Q: What was messy about it?**
A: Everything was separate — email, phone calls, and texts. I forgot follow-ups with schools that were actually interested.

**Q: What would help?**
A: A single place where conversations, film, and academic info live together.

## 4) Film & Profile Tools

**Q: What tools did you use for film/highlights?**
A: Hudl and YouTube. For goalkeepers I also had to send full games because coaches want to see positioning and communication.

**Q: Biggest issue?**
A: Every coach wants something different and you keep resending information repeatedly.

## 5) Concept Reaction

**Q: Would a single recruiting + highlight app help?**
A: Yes, especially for transfers where timing matters. Coaches are searching constantly and players need to be visible quickly.

**Q: What would make you trust it?**
A: Verified college coaches actually using it to scout players.
