## Key Qualitative Themes

### What Improved

1. **Headline landed hard (31 mentions):** "Stop Emailing Into the Void" was the single most-cited strongest line across all buckets. Athletes especially connected — "That's literally what it feels like." Transfer portal athletes had the strongest reaction.

2. **Clarity jumped significantly:** nailed_it went from 47% to 68%. The one-sentence subheadline did the work. Fewer personas confused Recruitr with a recruiting agency.

3. **Differentiation section helped:** The NCSA/Hudl comparison directly answered the most common V1 objection. "How is this different" dropped from 19 mentions to 6.

### What Still Feels Off

1. **"Reads like a SaaS landing page" (15 mentions):** The structure — hero, value props, differentiation, trust, CTA — feels formulaic to younger personas. HS athletes said it "looks like something my dad would sign up for." The tone is correct but the format feels corporate.

2. **D1 coaches remain skeptical (8/12 dealbreaker or neutral):** D1 programs already have Teamworks/ARMS. The value prop doesn't address their workflow. "We already have this. What does this do that Teamworks doesn't?" This bucket may be structurally unreachable for MVP.

3. **No visual or demo (11 mentions):** Coaches wanted to see the product. "Show me what the athlete profile looks like." "I'd rather see a 10-second screen recording than read about it." The copy improved but the page still feels text-heavy.

4. **"One profile, every coach" feels like a promise (9 mentions):** Athletes liked the phrase but questioned it. "Every coach? How many coaches are actually on this?" The phrasing creates an expectation the platform can't deliver yet.

## Recommendations for V3

1. **Add a product demo or video.** The single most requested element. A short screen recording or mock of the athlete profile and coach search would convert the "show me" crowd.
2. **Shift the tone younger.** The copy is accurate but reads corporate. Experiment with a more casual, social-media-native voice — especially in the hero.
3. **Consider dropping D1 coaches as a primary target.** 3 rounds of low engagement. They have enterprise tools. D2/D3/NAIA coaches are the real opportunity.
4. **Soften the "every coach" claim.** Replace with language about getting seen by the right programs rather than all programs.
5. **Reduce structural formality.** The hero → value props → differentiation → trust → CTA flow is predictable. Try a less segmented layout.
