# Changes from V1

- **Section:** Hero headline
  **Change:** Replaced "The Future of College Sports Recruiting Is Here" with "Stop Emailing Into the Void"
  **Why:** 23/60 personas flagged the original headline as generic startup copy. Using language directly from athlete interviews (Brandon Newman: "You send film but never know if a coach watched it") to lead with pain instead of hype.

- **Section:** Hero subheadline
  **Change:** Cut from 2 sentences (42 words) to 1 sentence (18 words). Removed "revolutionary," "innovative," and "technology-driven."
  **Why:** 12 personas said the page was too wordy and they'd bounce. Coach personas specifically rejected startup jargon.

- **Section:** Problem section
  **Change:** Removed entirely. Moved the strongest pain-point language into the hero.
  **Why:** The problem section was redundant with the hero. 14 personas stopped reading before reaching the solution section. Consolidating up.

- **Section:** Solution section
  **Change:** Replaced bullet-point feature lists with two short value prop blocks — one for athletes, one for coaches. 2 sentences each, no bullets.
  **Why:** 14 personas flagged bullet overload. Athletes under 20 disengaged at the feature lists. Shifting to benefit-oriented language.

- **Section:** Trust/differentiation
  **Change:** Added a line addressing the NCSA/SportsRecruits comparison directly: positioning Recruitr as the place where search, profiles, film, and messaging live together.
  **Why:** 19 personas asked "how is this different from NCSA?" — the single most common objection. Needs a direct answer.

- **Section:** Overall length
  **Change:** Cut total copy from ~280 words to ~150 words.
  **Why:** Responding to "too much text" feedback (18 mentions). Targeting a page that can be read in under 30 seconds.
