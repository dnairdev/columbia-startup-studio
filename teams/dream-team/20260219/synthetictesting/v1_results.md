## Key Qualitative Themes

### What Feels Off (most common themes)

1. **"Sounds like every other startup" (23 mentions):** The headline "The Future of College Sports Recruiting Is Here" was flagged as generic by nearly half of all personas. Multiple coach personas called it "AI-written" and "something I'd scroll past immediately."

2. **"Too much text, not enough proof" (18 mentions):** Athletes and coaches both noted the page is heavy on promises and light on evidence. "You're telling me you solve everything but I have no reason to believe you." Several coach personas specifically asked for social proof or numbers.

3. **"Bullet point overload" (14 mentions):** The feature lists for athletes and coaches read like a spec sheet, not a landing page. "I stopped reading at the third bullet point." Athletes under 20 were especially likely to disengage here.

4. **"Who is actually on this?" (12 mentions):** The biggest trust gap. Coaches wanted to know which programs are already using it. Athletes wanted to know if real coaches would actually see their profile. "Another platform means nothing if coaches aren't there."

### Strongest Lines

- "No fake accounts, no spam" — resonated with 11 personas (mostly athletes)
- "Minutes instead of hours" — resonated with 8 personas (mostly coaches)

### Top Objections

- "How is this different from NCSA or SportsRecruits?" (19 mentions)
- "I don't see any coaches/athletes on here" (15 mentions)
- "This is too wordy, I'd bounce" (12 mentions)
- "The headline is generic" (11 mentions)

### Dealbreaker Reasons

- No evidence of real coach adoption (14 personas)
- Sounds identical to existing platforms (9 personas)

Common misunderstanding: 7 personas thought Recruitr was a recruiting agency or service rather than a self-serve platform. 3 thought it was only for football.

---

## Recommendations for V2

1. **Kill the generic headline.** Replace with something specific and emotionally resonant. The "future of X is here" framing is dead on arrival.
2. **Cut the copy in half.** This page tries to say everything. Pick one angle and commit.
3. **Lead with the pain, not the solution.** Athletes and coaches connected most when the copy described problems they recognize. The solution section felt like a feature dump.
4. **Add a differentiator.** Multiple personas compared this unfavorably to NCSA/SportsRecruits. The copy doesn't explain what makes Recruitr different.
5. **Show, don't list.** Replace the bullet points with a visual or demo. Coaches specifically said they'd rather see the product than read about it.
