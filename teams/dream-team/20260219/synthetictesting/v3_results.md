## Key Qualitative Themes

### What Improved

1. **Massive jump with HS athletes (+17pp resonance, +17pp conversion):** The social media framing and minimal layout resonated. "This actually looks like something I'd use." "It's not trying to be LinkedIn, that's good." The tone shift was the single biggest driver.

2. **Demo video converted the "show me" crowd:** 9 personas who were dealbreakers in V2 moved to neutral or positive in V3, citing the demo. "Now I can see what this actually is." Coaches especially responded — "Video-first makes sense, I can evaluate faster."

3. **"LinkedIn meets TikTok" landed (22 mentions as strongest line):** This framing immediately communicated the concept. Personas who previously scored "partial" on clarity now scored "nailed_it." The analogy does heavy lifting.

4. **Transfer athletes are the strongest segment (67% resonance, 50% conversion, 8% dealbreaker):** This group consistently scores highest. They have the most acute pain and the fewest existing tools. Strong signal for go-to-market targeting.

### What Still Feels Off

1. **Coach value tagline doesn't match the vibe (13 mentions):** "Find real prospects in minutes, not months" was called out as "generic SaaS copy" by 8 personas. It clashes with the casual, social-media-native tone of the rest of the page. "The top of the page feels cool, then this line feels like a sales pitch."

2. **D1 coaches still not converting (33% resonance, 17% conversion):** Consistent with V1 and V2. D1 programs have Teamworks/ARMS and dedicated recruiting staff. This segment appears structurally unreachable at MVP stage.

3. **"Phase 1 MVP" in the footer (7 mentions):** Several personas flagged this as undermining trust. "If you're telling me this is an MVP, why would I sign up?" "That's an internal label, not something you put on a landing page."

4. **Missing social proof (9 mentions):** The page is clean but has zero evidence of traction. "How many people are on this?" "Any testimonials?" The minimal design works but the page could use one proof point.

## Recommendations for V4

1. **Rewrite the coach value tagline.** Keep it consistent with the casual, social-media-native tone. Focus on the evaluation speed angle ("video-first profiles coaches can evaluate in seconds") rather than the generic "minutes not months" framing.
2. **Consider replacing D1 coaches bucket.** Three rounds of low conversion with structural dealbreakers. Replace with a "Sports Parents" bucket or expand the transfer athlete bucket.
3. **Remove "Phase 1 MVP" from footer** or replace with something neutral like just "Recruitr."
4. **Add one proof point** — even a waitlist count or number of athlete profiles. Something concrete.
