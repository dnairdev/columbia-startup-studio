# Changes from V3

- **Section:** Coach value tagline
  **Change:** Replaced "Find real prospects in minutes, not months. Search, watch, and message — all in one place." with "Recruiting, reimagined: video-first profiles coaches can actually evaluate in seconds."
  **Why:** 13 personas flagged the old tagline as generic SaaS copy that clashed with the casual tone of the rest of the page. The new line keeps the evaluation-speed insight (from coach interviews — Tobias Esche described a "first 30 seconds" filter) but frames it in a way that matches the page's voice.

- **Section:** Footer
  **Change:** Kept "Phase 1 MVP" label for now — it signals transparency and early-access positioning.
  **Why:** 7 personas flagged it negatively, but the early-access framing is deliberate. Testing whether keeping it with "Recruitr • Phase 1 MVP" reads as honest transparency rather than lack of polish. Will remove if V4 feedback confirms it's still hurting.

- **Section:** Demo section
  **Change:** Added "Coach Mode" and "Coach demo" labels above the demo video to signal the coach-side experience.
  **Why:** V3 feedback showed coach personas wanted to see their workflow specifically. Labeling the demo as coach-facing sets expectations and addresses the "what does the coach side look like" question before they ask it.

- **Section:** Audience bucket swap
  **Change:** Replaced D1 Coaches bucket with Sports Parents bucket (12 personas — parents of HS juniors/seniors actively involved in the recruiting process).
  **Why:** D1 coaches showed 17% conversion or lower across 3 consecutive rounds with structural dealbreakers (they already have Teamworks/ARMS). 33% dealbreaker rate was consistent. This segment is not reachable at MVP. Sports parents are a reachable adjacent segment — they're often the ones managing the recruiting process and making tool decisions.
