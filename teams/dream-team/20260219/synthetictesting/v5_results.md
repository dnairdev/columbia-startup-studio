## Key Findings

### Convergence Confirmed

Two consecutive rounds (V4→V5) with minimal movement across all dimensions. The copy has reached its ceiling for this audience without real-world traction signals (testimonials, user counts, case studies). Further synthetic iteration is unlikely to produce meaningful gains.

### Transfer Athletes Are the Strongest Segment

83% resonance, 67% conversion, 0% dealbreaker. This group should be the primary GTM target. They have the most urgent pain (time-sensitive portal process), the least existing tooling, and the highest willingness to try a new platform.

### Five Lines That Carried the Page

| Line | Total mentions as "strongest line" (across V3–V5) |
|------|----------------------------------------------------|
| "The LinkedIn of sports meets TikTok" | 67 |
| "Recruiting, reimagined: video-first profiles coaches can actually evaluate in seconds" | 46 |
| "Recruiting, simplified" | 39 |
| "feels like social media — because that's where the talent already is" | 31 |
| "No spam. Just early access updates." | 22 |

### Objections the Copy Couldn't Fully Resolve

These are structural objections that require product traction, not better copy:

1. **"How many coaches/athletes are actually on here?"** — Cannot be resolved without real user count. Consider adding a waitlist counter or "25+ athlete profiles" once the number is defensible.
2. **"Is this only for soccer?"** — The demo video shows soccer. If multi-sport is the intent, the demo needs to reflect that. Copy alone can't fix a visual mismatch.
3. **"I'd need to see a coach I recognize using it"** — Social proof from a known coach would unlock the remaining skeptics. This is a GTM milestone, not a copy fix.

## Final Recommendations

1. **Ship this copy.** V5 has crossed the 50% conversion threshold, dealbreakers are at 10%, and two consecutive rounds show convergence. Further synthetic iteration will not meaningfully improve performance.
2. **Target transfer portal athletes first in GTM.** Strongest segment by every metric. Highest urgency, lowest dealbreaker rate.
3. **Add a proof point when available.** Even a modest number ("50+ athlete profiles and counting") would address the remaining trust gap that copy alone can't close.
4. **Expand the demo to show multi-sport** if the product supports it. The soccer-specific demo is creating a scope objection with club coaches in other sports.
5. **Collect real testimonials early.** Kevin Wehner (St. John's MBB) offered to help with the prototype. A quote from a real coach would resolve the strongest remaining objection.
