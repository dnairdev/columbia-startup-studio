# Product Brief

**Team Name:** Recruitr
**Date:** February 19, 2026

---

## The Problem

College soccer recruiting is fragmented, opaque, and overwhelming for both sides. Athletes send hundreds of cold emails with highlight reels attached and have no idea if a single coach ever watched their film. Coaches drown in 250+ recruiting emails per day and rely on a patchwork of Hudl, YouTube, spreadsheets, NCSA, text threads, and Instagram DMs to evaluate and track prospects. There is no single place where film, communication, academic info, and recruiting status live together.

- **Who has this problem?** College soccer athletes trying to get recruited (especially D2/D3/NAIA/JUCO-level and transfer portal athletes), college coaches evaluating prospects, and club coaches/scouts helping players navigate the process.
- **How do they currently deal with it?** Athletes use some combination of email, Hudl, YouTube, SportsRecruits, NCSA, iMovie, Notes app, spreadsheets, and physical notebooks. Coaches use Teamworks (ARMS), Hudl, YouTube, email, and spreadsheets. Everyone has their own system and nothing talks to anything else.
- **How painful is it?** Very. Athletes described sending film "into a void" with zero feedback. One interviewee forgot follow-ups with schools that were actually interested. A club scout said he'd see a player months later and not remember his own evaluation. A youth coach said talented international players miss out entirely because they don't understand the process. Multiple interviewees were spending money on recruiting services, film editing, and platform subscriptions — and still felt lost.

---

## The Solution

Recruitr is a recruiting platform where college athletes build a single profile — bio, stats, academics, highlight reel — and college coaches search, filter, and message them directly. It replaces the scattered workflow of emails, spreadsheets, and multiple video platforms with one centralized place for discovery and connection.

- **What does it do?** Athletes create a profile and go live. Coaches search by sport, position, grad year, region, and GPA. Coaches message athletes directly through the platform.
- **How does the user interact with it?** Athletes build a profile once and toggle it to "active." Coaches search, review profiles, save shortlists, and reach out. Both sides manage conversations in one place.
- **What makes it different?** Unlike Hudl (film only), NCSA (database + mass email), or SportsRecruits (profile hosting), Recruitr combines the athlete profile, film, search/discovery, and coach-athlete messaging in a single workflow. Coaches are verified, so athletes know who's reaching out.

---

## Target User

**Primary — Athletes:**
- **Demographics:** 16–22 years old, high school juniors/seniors and college transfers, playing club or high school soccer, spread across the U.S.
- **Defining behavior:** Actively trying to get recruited — sending cold emails to coaches, making highlight reels, managing spreadsheets of programs they've contacted.
- **How they describe the problem:** "You send film but never know if a coach watched it." / "Everything was separate — email, phone calls, and texts. I forgot follow-ups with schools that were actually interested." / "A lot of cold emails and hard getting initial interest from coaches."

**Secondary — Coaches:**
- **Demographics:** College coaches at D1–D3, NAIA, and JUCO programs, plus club directors and scouts.
- **Defining behavior:** Evaluating dozens of prospects per month across multiple platforms, managing recruiting boards, losing follow-ups.
- **How they describe the problem:** "The system is only as good as the data you put in and it can be tedious." / "Everything lives in different places. That's the annoying part."

---

## Why Now

The transfer portal has exploded college recruiting volume — coaches now actively scout transfers in addition to high schoolers, and athletes in the portal need to move fast. At the same time, the tools haven't kept up: Hudl is for film, NCSA is for databases, and everything else lives in email and spreadsheets. Young athletes already create and share video content natively. The infrastructure (cloud video, real-time messaging, search indexing) is cheap and mature. The gap between how recruiting works and how it should work has never been wider.

---

## Key Interview Evidence

- **Strongest quote:** "You send film but never know if a coach watched it." — Brandon Newman, D1 midfielder at Cal Poly SLO
- **Pattern:** 7 of 9 interviewees described fragmented communication (email + text + DMs + spreadsheets) as a core pain point. Organization breaks down when prospects or programs multiply.
- **Surprise:** A D1 assistant coach receives ~250 recruiting emails per day — the sheer volume means most athlete outreach is never seen. Also, the transfer portal has become a dominant channel, creating a new urgent use case.
- **Current spend/effort:** Multiple athletes paid for NCSA, SportsRecruits, ConnectSoccer, and professional highlight editing services. One athlete (Cy Sokolowski) used five different platforms and still found the process tedious. Club coaches spend hours helping players write emails and prepare videos.
