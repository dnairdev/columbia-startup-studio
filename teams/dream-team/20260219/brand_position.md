# Brand Position

**Team Name:** Recruitr
**Date:** February 19, 2026

**Purpose:** This is an internal document. It captures our brand's thesis, identity, and canonical language so that everything we build — landing page, copy, features, pitch — speaks with one voice. This is not marketing copy. It is a decision reference.

---

## Brand Thesis

> **Recruiting should reward talent, not connections.**

---

## Core Product Definition

> **Recruitr helps college athletes and coaches find each other by putting profiles, film, and messaging in one place — replacing the scattered mess of emails, spreadsheets, and highlight links.**

---

## Existence Statement

> **Recruitr exists to make sure talented athletes don't get overlooked just because they didn't know the right people or understand the process.**

---

## Target Identity

> **Recruitr is for someone who believes that where you train shouldn't determine whether you get seen — that a midfielder in Denver deserves the same shot as one in a coach's backyard.**

---

## Canonical Language

- **"Get seen."** — The core athlete promise. You shouldn't have to send 200 cold emails to get noticed.
- **"One profile. Every coach."** — Build it once, be discoverable everywhere.
- **"Stop emailing into the void."** — Directly from athlete interviews. The current process is a black hole.
- **"Recruiting should be simple."** — Not a grind across five platforms and a spreadsheet.
- **"Real coaches, real interest."** — Verified coaches, transparent engagement. No guessing.

---

## Language to Avoid

- **Avoid:** "AI-powered" or "smart algorithm" — **Because:** It overpromises on tech we don't have yet and sounds like every other startup. We're solving a workflow problem, not a data science problem.
- **Avoid:** "Disrupt" or "revolutionize" — **Because:** Recruiting isn't broken because it lacks innovation. It's broken because it's fragmented. We're consolidating, not disrupting.
- **Avoid:** "Network" or "social platform" — **Because:** We're not building another social feed. Coaches in our interviews were clear: "It can't just be another highlight feed. It has to add structure."
- **Avoid:** "Exposure" (on its own) — **Because:** NCSA and SportsRecruits already promise exposure. Our value is the connection that comes after exposure — the message, the reply, the follow-up.

---

## Tone Guidelines

- **Direct not hype-y** — Say what the product does. Don't oversell. Athletes and coaches are skeptical of platforms that promise everything.
- **Confident not corporate** — We're a small team that talks to users every week. Write like that, not like a press release.
- **Empathetic not patronizing** — Athletes going through recruiting are stressed. Coaches are overwhelmed. Acknowledge the grind without pretending we magically fix everything.
- **Specific not vague** — Use real numbers, real quotes, real scenarios. "250 emails a day" beats "coaches are busy."

---

## Positioning Statement

> **For college athletes and coaches in soccer, Recruitr is the recruiting platform that turns scattered emails and highlight links into a single searchable profile and direct messaging system, because the current process buries talent under inbox noise and spreadsheet chaos.**

---

## Brand Values

- **Simplicity over features** — If a feature adds complexity without solving a clear pain point from interviews, we cut it. The product should feel lighter than what coaches and athletes use today, not heavier.
- **Transparency over polish** — We'd rather show athletes which coaches viewed their profile than build a prettier highlight player. Real information beats aesthetics.
- **Access over exclusivity** — The whole point is that a D3 athlete in a small town gets the same shot as a showcase kid. Design decisions should widen the funnel, not narrow it.

---

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Our Response |
|-----------|--------------|
| "I already use NCSA / SportsRecruits / Hudl — why switch?" | Those tools do one thing well (database, profiles, or film). None of them combine search, profiles, film, and messaging in one place. You're still bouncing between tabs and spreadsheets. Recruitr is the single workflow. |
| "How do I know real coaches are on here?" | Every coach account is manually verified before they can message athletes. You'll see their school, sport, and division. No anonymous accounts, no fake interest. |
| "Another recruiting platform? What makes this different?" | We're not a highlight feed and we're not a database. We're the place where the actual connection happens — coach finds athlete, coach messages athlete, athlete responds. One workflow, start to finish. |

## Key Interview Language

1. **"You send film but never know if a coach watched it."** — Brandon Newman, D1 midfielder. The black-hole problem. This is the emotional core of the athlete experience.
2. **"Everything lives in different places. That's the annoying part."** — Kevin Wehner, St. John's MBB. Tool fragmentation from the coach/staff side.
3. **"Everything was separate — email, phone calls, and texts. I forgot follow-ups with schools that were actually interested."** — Dean Kelliher, D1 goalkeeper. The organizational breakdown during transfer recruiting.
4. **"Many talented players don't get recruited simply because they don't understand the process or don't reach the right coaches."** — Durval Pereira, youth head coach. The access gap, especially for international players.
5. **"It can't just be another highlight feed. It has to add structure."** — Kevin Wehner. The bar we have to clear. Structure is the differentiator.
