# Weekly update — Circles — 2026-04-21

## What we built last week (and why)

**Product**

- **Columbia Events integration:** Imported all upcoming Columbia events via the Bedework feed and added a dedicated Columbia Events section directly to the Home feed—solving the "empty room" problem and providing immediate value.
- **Smart search bar:** Added a typo-tolerant search bar with Columbia-specific filters, while refining search result layouts (e.g., removing thumbnail images to emphasize text).
- **Social system and discovery:** Built out a complete social layer with named, deep-linked notifications, a friends graph, and a new Friends tab on the mobile bottom navigation.
- **Guest browsing:** Enabled guest browsing with sign-in prompts for protected actions to lower the barrier to entry for new users.

**Polish and Quality Bar**

- **Visual rebranding and typography:** Transitioned branding to "Chords", improved overall typography, and standardized avatar backgrounds (including deterministic placeholder colors and app-wide profile photos instead of initials).
- **Circle Details:** Upgraded to Collage Cards where member-contributed photos replace the creator's face as the cover, added a circle photo gallery, and relocated the share button for stronger contrast.
- **Mobile and layout fixes:** Rebuilt the right sidebar, prevented horizontal scrolling on mobile, properly centered the bottom navigation FAB, and standardized the LIVE badge placement.
- **Feed management:** Revamped the activity feed with limits, overlap detection, and "view more/less" toggles.

**Growth and Feedback**

- **Columbia Subreddit launch:** We posted our application on the Columbia subreddit to start driving organic campus traffic.
- **Early feedback:** We received strong validation from students on Reddit regarding our value proposition: *"This looks super cool! It’s great to see all campus events in a single place instead of sifting through emails or reading posters on campus trying to find what’s happening on campus. Is this going to be released in app form soon?"*

**Why (summary):** We focused on populating the app with real campus events so users immediately see value without needing an existing network. Concurrently, we polished the social and discovery systems so that as users arrive from Reddit, they can easily explore, browse as guests, and connect with peers.
