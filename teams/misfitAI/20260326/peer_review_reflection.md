# Peer Review Reflection

## What worked

During peer review, people understood that our app was related to fashion and wardrobe planning. They liked the overall idea of using technology to help organize clothing and generate outfits. The most promising part of the product was the vision-based feature, because it felt more unique and closer to the main purpose of the app.

## What confused people or broke

The biggest issue from peer review was that some people were confused about what the app actually did at first. In particular, the manual wardrobe flow made the product feel less clear, because peers were not sure whether the app was mainly for entering clothes by hand, searching for items, or using AI vision to identify clothing. We also heard that the wording around wardrobe inputs, especially "type" in the wardrobe section, was unclear. Another point of confusion was that "Search & Add" and "Manual" felt too similar, so users did not always understand why both options existed separately.

## Biggest gap

The biggest gap is clarity in onboarding and first impression. Our app needs to communicate its main value immediately: this is an AI wardrobe planner that can help users build a digital closet and generate outfits. Right now, that purpose is not obvious enough when users first interact with the wardrobe-adding flow.

## Improvement based on peer review

Based on the feedback, the most important improvement is to make the vision feature the primary first step and clarify the wording in the wardrobe flow. Leading with vision better shows what makes the app special, while clearer labels can reduce confusion about what users are supposed to enter. We would also simplify the experience by treating "Search & Add" and "Manual" as more closely related actions instead of presenting them as completely separate ideas.

## Reflection

This peer review was useful because it showed us that a strong idea is not enough if users do not understand the product right away. We learned that our interface needs to guide users more clearly and emphasize the feature that makes our app stand out. The feedback helped us see that our current design puts too much burden on the user to figure out the workflow for themselves.

Moving forward, we want to focus on clearer onboarding, better wording, and a more obvious starting point. If users immediately understand that the app can use vision to help build a digital wardrobe, they are more likely to see the value of the product. Overall, the peer review helped us identify that our biggest challenge is not the concept itself, but how clearly we present that concept to new users.
