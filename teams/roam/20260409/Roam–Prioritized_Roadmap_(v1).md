# Product Roadmap — Roam

**Product:** Roam
**Date:** March 26, 2026
**Demo Day:** May 12, 2026

**#1 Priority:** Find the Right User + Validate Retention

---

## Where We Are Now

Right now, the core problem is signal vs. noise.

### We have

**What's working:**

- An MVP people think is “cool”

**What's not working / biggest gap:**

- No strong pull, no one asking for access
- Messy usage data
- No clear ICP (ideal customer profile)

This means our priority is:
Validate who actually needs Roam and whether it creates repeat behavior.

---

## What We Need to Build

### What We’re Building First (Next 2–3 Weeks)

### 1) Clean + Instrument Data Properly

**Goal:** Understand real behavior, not assumptions.

**Define key events:**

- Create pin
- Save location
- Return session (Day 1, Day 7)
- Share or export map
- Clean current dataset and remove unusable logs
- Set up a simple analytics dashboard

**Output:**

Clear answers to:

- Are people coming back?
- What actions correlate with retention?

### 2) Tighten the Core Loop (Retention Loop)

**Goal:** Make Roam something people naturally come back to

**Focus on one loop:**

- Save → Organize → Revisit → Add more

**Ship improvements like:**

- Faster pinning with less friction
- Simple organization such as folders or tags
- Visual reward so the map improves over time
- No new features. Only strengthen this loop.

### 3) Identify + Test a Specific User Segment

**Goal:** Stop building for everyone

**Start with 2 to 3 hypotheses:**

- Aesthetic map curators
- Travel planners or frequent travelers
- NYC or urban explorers

**Actions:**

- Manually recruit 10 to 15 users per segment
- Observe how they use it or do not use it
- Track retention by group

**Output:**

- One segment shows strong pull or we reconsider the idea

### 4) Lightweight Distribution Experiment (Inspired by Tabi)

Instead of launching, test for pull:

**TikTok or Reels:**

- “My saved NYC spots map”
- “Hidden gems I’ve pinned”
- Shareable public maps
- Aesthetic and identity-driven content

**Goal:**

See if anyone says:

- “I want this”

### What We’re Cutting (For Now)

**New feature expansion:**

- Social features
- Complex collaboration
- AI recommendations
- Major UI redesigns

**Premature scaling:**

- No full launch
- No paid growth

**Over-engineering:**

- No infrastructure work beyond basics

**Rule:**
If it does not improve retention or user clarity, we do not build it.

### What’s Deferred

- Full mobile app polish
- Social graph or following system
- Monetization
- Advanced search or discovery
- Integrations such as Google Maps sync

These only matter after we confirm users care and return.

---

## Demo Day Vision

### Success Metrics (Next Milestone)

We move forward only if we see:

- At least 30 percent Day 1 retention in a specific segment
- At least 15 percent Day 7 retention
- Users organically sharing with others
- At least a few users asking when they can access it
