# Vouch — Check-in · March 26, 2026

---

## Look Back: Peer Review

**What worked when people used your product?**

The one-tap rating loop clicked immediately — low friction, easy habit. "Like Beli but for everything" landed as a shortcut for people who knew Beli. The taste profile felt personal and ownable even when sparse. People also liked that the app was useful solo before any friends joined.

**What confused them or broke?**

The Vouch vs. Beli distinction wasn't obvious enough — people kept asking "what does this do that Beli doesn't?" The social layer felt abstract when your network is empty. A few people didn't know what counted as rateable (a gym class? a one-drink rooftop stop?). The Pro tier value wasn't concrete — "taste analytics" doesn't mean anything until you see it.

**What's the biggest gap?**

The social graph is the product but it's treated like a feature. Right now Vouch reads as a personal log that also has friends. It should flip: Vouch is a trust network, and the personal log is just the on-ramp. There's no designed moment where the network visibly does something for you.

**What do you still need to learn?**

Who's the real first audience — the "experienced NYC person" who already has strong opinions and a mental map of the city, or the college/campus user who's very network-dense and share-happy? These are different retention loops and different onboarding paths. We also don't know what the minimum viable network size is before the social feed feels genuinely alive. And we haven't confirmed whether the compounding promise ("month three is dramatically more useful than day one") actually holds in practice.

**One improvement shipped based on what we heard**

Reframed the social layer on the landing page from passive to active. Changed "your friends are rating too" → "the people you trust are your best algorithm." Made the Vouch vs. Beli distinction explicit: Beli ranks by crowd consensus, Vouch shows you what your actual people loved — across everything.

## Look Forward: Your Roadmap

**What do you need to build between now and Demo Day?**

1. A shareable profile card that looks good in iMessage/WhatsApp — this is the demo moment and the main acquisition mechanic
2. A designed aha moment path: rate 3 things → prompt to share profile → friend views it → friend rates → you get notified
3. Friend activity email so people actually come back (currently the only re-engagement is remembering the app exists)
4. One fully built Neighbourhood Guide for a real NYC neighborhood — something a Demo Day judge could open and use tonight
5. Campus/community outreach: seed one dense mini-network (10+ mutually-following active users) before May 12. 30 real users who follow each other beats 300 strangers. Target: a college friend group, a fitness class, or a food-obsessed group chat.

**What's your team's biggest open question?**

Is the loop social-first or personal-first — and does that change how we do onboarding and outreach? If we go hard on campus seeding (Version B: social-first), the product needs to deliver the network magic fast or that cohort churns before they tell anyone. We don't know yet what the threshold is.

**What does success look like on May 12?**

One person in the audience says "I already texted my friend about this" — not "cool idea." Concretely: at least one real mini-network using the app, a profile share that looks good enough to screenshot, a live Discover Bar query that actually works, and a Wrapped card that makes someone say "I want that in 6 months."
