## INTRO (read this verbatim)

> "Thanks for helping us out today. I'm going to have you try to use our product. A few things before we start:
>
> Please think out loud as you go — say whatever's on your mind. Whatever you're looking at, whatever you're wondering, say it.
>
> I won't help you or answer questions during the test. This might feel awkward, but it's important — we want to see how the product performs, not how well I can explain it.
>
> There are no wrong answers. We're testing the product, not you. If something is confusing, that's valuable information for us.
>
> We'll be recording the audio and screen portion of this so that we can review again later. That was noted in the informed consent document that you signed, but I just want to mention it again.
>
> Any questions before we start?"

## TASKS

### Task 1 — First impression
**Say:** "Go ahead and open siftapp.site. Take a look at the page and tell me what you think this product does."

*Let them look for 30–60 seconds. Don't explain anything.*

What to watch for:
- Do they understand from the headline alone that this helps them find things to do in NYC?
- Do they mention the problem (too many apps, missing events, decision fatigue) unprompted?
- What words do they use to describe it? Write these down verbatim.
- Does anything confuse them or seem out of place?

---

### Task 2 — Sign up
**Say:** "Go ahead and try to sign up."

*Don't help. Let them struggle.*

What to watch for:
- Do they find the sign-up CTA immediately or look around?
- Where do they pause or hesitate?
- Do they complete the flow or drop off?
- Any friction with the form fields or the process?

---

### Task 3 — Core flow
**Say:** "Now try to find something you'd want to do in NYC this weekend."

*Don't help. Let them go through the quiz on their own.*

What to watch for:
- Do the 3 questions make sense without explanation?
- Do they hesitate on any specific question (vibe, time, distance)?
- Do the recommendations feel relevant to what they answered?
- What's the first moment they seem to "get it" — or do they ever?
- Do they click into a recommendation or stop at the list?

---

### Task 4 — Discovery moment
**Say:** "Is there anything in the results you wouldn't have found on your own?"

*Let them explore. Don't prompt further.*

What to watch for:
- Do they react to any recommendation with surprise or genuine interest?
- Do they say anything like "I didn't know this existed" or "how did you find this?"
- Do the results feel like real NYC things or generic?

---

## POST-SESSION QUESTIONS

After tasks are complete, have a brief conversation. These are open-ended — let them talk.

1. "What was the first moment you felt like you understood what Sift is for?"

2. "What was the most confusing part of the experience?"

3. "What would have made it easier?"

4. "Is there anything you expected to find that wasn't there?"

5. "Would you actually use this on a Friday or Saturday when you're figuring out your weekend?"

6. "How does this compare to how you currently find things to do in NYC?"

7. "If you could change one thing about Sift, what would it be?"
