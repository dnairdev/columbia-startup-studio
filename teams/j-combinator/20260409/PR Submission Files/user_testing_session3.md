## Problem Definition

- The core problem is well-identified: people ask "what should I do this weekend?", bounce between Instagram, TikTok, Google Maps, and search results, and still can't make a decision.
- However, the solution hypothesis is too broad/general — the app needs a sharper, more specific angle to stand out.

## Onboarding & UX

- The app name "Sift" lacks intuitive clarity — it's not immediately obvious what the app does.
- The onboarding survey is too long. Having a heavy questionnaire at login, then another layer of preference-setting on the discover page, feels redundant and will drive up bounce rates significantly.
- Recommended fix: remove the onboarding survey entirely and instead introduce filters organically on the discover page.
- The default landing should be the discover page in guest mode — users shouldn't have to log in just to see what the app offers. Getting to the "aha moment" as fast as possible is critical.

## Differentiation & Value Proposition

- The service value alone isn't enough to differentiate — curating/listing events well is table stakes, not a moat.
- In-app booking/reservation is a high-friction feature that users are unlikely to adopt (e.g., MyRealTrip tried it and people didn't use it — they still book through individual platforms).
- There needs to be at least one "only possible here" feature to anchor the value prop.

## Saving / Planning Feature

- The saving feature has real potential — people constantly save things on Instagram and forget them, then miss out on the weekend.
- A well-integrated save-and-resurface mechanism (e.g., "you saved this place — it's nearby this weekend") could be a strong differentiator.
- The Plan page as currently conceived (booking/itinerary building) is low-value since users already manage plans via their own calendar or spreadsheet tools.

## Funnel Priority

- The discover page is make-or-break: if 90 out of 100 users drop off there, even a 10% plan conversion = 1 user. Fix the top of the funnel first.
- Front-end of the customer journey is almost always more important than downstream conversion.

## Strategic Suggestions

- Narrow the hypothesis dramatically and validate quickly rather than building broadly.
- Consider targeting a specific niche: e.g., early adopters/hipsters looking for non-mainstream spots — a tighter audience with a clearer value prop.
- Look at viral vibe-coded apps like "Broke Map" — a Korean map-based budget restaurant finder — as an example of a sharply focused, instantly understandable product with a strong name and clear aha moment.
- Dig deeper into *why* people can't decide what to do on weekends — different users likely have different root causes, and the solution should match a specific one.
