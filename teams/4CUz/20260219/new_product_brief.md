**The Problem**

Planning trips with friends is often chaotic and time-consuming because coordination happens across multiple apps and group chats.

Students struggle to align schedules, choose destinations, decide on activities, and split expenses fairly. As group size increases, planning becomes increasingly disorganized, and one person usually ends up doing most of the work.

Group chats quickly become cluttered with links, messages, and partial decisions, making it difficult to track what has been decided and what still needs agreement.

* Who has this problem?  
  * College students, graduate students, and young professionals who frequently plan trips with friends, including:  
    * Daytrips/ Weekend trips  
    * Spring/Winter break travel  
    * Post-Semester vacations  
    * Group Visits to nearby cities or events

* How do they currently deal with it?

  * Group Chats

  * Shared Google Docs/documents

  * Informal Polls

  * Airbnb and flight links sent through messages or emails

  * Splitwise/ Notes App/ Spreadsheet for tracking expenses

* How painful is it? (What evidence do you have from interviews?)

  * Interviewees frequently described group trip planning as messy, slow, and frustrating, particularly when trying to coordinate schedules and decisions across multiple people.

  * Several interviewees mentioned that planning takes significantly longer than expected and that decisions often stall because not everyone responds at the same time.

  * In many cases, one person ends up managing logistics for the entire group, leading to uneven workload and frustration.

---

**The Solution**

We are building a Group Trip Planner, a collaborative platform designed specifically for planning trips with friends.

The app centralizes the entire planning process in one place by allowing groups to:

* Highlight shared availability through a group calendar  
* Vote on destinations and activities  
* Organize itineraries collaboratively  
* Track and split shared expenses automatically

Instead of juggling multiple tools and messy group chats, the app provides a structured environment where decisions can be made clearly and efficiently.

Unlike existing tools, which address only one part of the process (polling, expenses, or messaging), our solution integrates the entire group planning workflow into a single platform.  
---

**Target User**

* Demographics:   
  * College, graduate students, and young professionals (18–30) who frequently travel with friends and coordinate plans digitally.  
* Defining behavior:   
  * Plans or participates in group trips with friends  
  * Uses group chats to coordinate logistics  
  * Shares links for flights, accommodations, and activities in messaging apps  
  * Has experienced difficulty coordinating schedules or decisions in a group  
* How they describe the problem:  
  * “Planning the trip is harder than the trip itself.”  
  * “The group chat gets so messy that we lose track of what we decided.”  
  * “Someone always ends up doing all the planning.”

---

**Why Now**

Several conditions make this problem newly salient:

* Group travel among young adults is becoming more common, particularly for weekend trips, festivals, and shared vacations.  
* Coordination still happens primarily through unstructured group chats, which are not designed for organizing decisions or logistics.  
* There is no widely adopted platform that simplifies group travel planning specifically

Advances in mobile collaboration tools and shared planning interfaces create an opportunity to streamline this process into a single structured workflow.
