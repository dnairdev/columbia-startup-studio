# **Brand Thesis**

Group trips fall apart not because people don’t want to travel together — but because coordinating schedules, budgets, destinations, and activities across multiple people becomes chaotic.

**Trip planning should feel like the start of an adventure, not a project manager’s job.**

Tripable exists to transform chaotic group trip coordination into a shared, collaborative experience where ideas turn into plans quickly and naturally.

---

# **Core Product Definition**

**Tripable helps groups plan trips by bringing the entire planning process into one place. It lets groups find overlapping availability, suggest and vote on destinations and activities, generate a realistic itinerary, and track shared expenses. Instead of juggling multiple apps and conversations, it turns scattered ideas into a clear plan that everyone helps create.**

---

# **Existence Statement**

**Tripable exists to remove the hidden labor of group trip planning so that no single person has to carry the mental load of organizing everything.**

---

# **Target Identity**

**Tripable is for college students, graduate students, and young professionals who frequently travel with friends and currently organize trips through group chats and shared links.**

Or more broadly:

**Tripable is for someone who loves traveling with friends but hates the messy coordination that comes before the trip.**

These phrases should appear frequently across product surfaces.

* **Plan trips together**  
* **Everyone gets a say**  
* **Decide as a group**  
* **Find dates that work for everyone**  
* **Turn ideas into a shared plan**  
* **From suggestions to itinerary**  
* **Planning without the chaos**

---

# **Language to Avoid**

* **Avoid:** “trip management software” — **Because:** sounds corporate and removes the emotional context of travel.  
* **Avoid:** “project planning” — **Because:** planning a vacation should not feel like work.  
* **Avoid:** “task tracking” — **Because:** implies productivity tooling rather than collaborative travel.

---

# **Tone Guidelines**

* **Friendly not corporate** — travel is social and exciting, not operational.  
* **Simple not technical** — focus on outcomes, not algorithms or logistics.  
* **Collaborative not directive** — emphasize group participation rather than one user controlling everything.  
* **Calm not chaotic** — planning feels organized and effortless.  
* **Exploratory not transactional** — planning should feel like discovering possibilities, not completing tasks.

---

# **Positioning Statement**

**For groups of friends planning trips together,Tripable is the collaborative trip planning platform that turns group ideas into a shared itinerary. Unlike group chats, scattered polls, and spreadsheets, the product brings scheduling, destination decisions, activity planning, and expense tracking into one collaborative space designed specifically for group travel.**

---

# **Brand Values**

**1\. Collaboration over control**  
Trips should be shaped by the group, not managed by one person responsible for organizing everything.

**2\. Reduce planning friction**  
Every feature should replace messy conversations, scattered links, and unclear decisions with simple shared interactions.

**3\. Clarity over complexity**  
Planning tools should make decisions visible and organized so groups always know what has been decided and what still needs input.

**4\. Planning should feel social**  
Trip planning should feel like part of the travel experience — exciting, interactive, and collaborative.

---

# **Objection Handling**

| Objection | Your Response |
| ----- | ----- |
| "We already just use a group chat or Google Doc." | Group chats are great for conversation but terrible for decisions. The product turns scattered suggestions into clear choices and a final itinerary. |
| "Planning trips together is already easy." | It is easy when one person ends up doing all the work. The product distributes decisions so no one has to coordinate everything. |
| "Voting sounds slow." | Voting actually speeds things up by replacing endless back-and-forth discussion with quick consensus. |

# **Key Interview Language**

1. **"I always end up being the one who organizes everything."** — common frustration among friend groups.  
2. **"Our group chat has like 200 messages and we still haven't decided anything."** — decision paralysis in group travel planning.  
3. **"We had so many ideas but no actual plan."** — lack of structure between suggestions and itinerary.  
4. **"We spent more time planning than actually enjoying the trip."** — planning fatigue.
