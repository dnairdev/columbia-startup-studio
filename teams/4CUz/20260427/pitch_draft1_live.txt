TRIPABLE
Group trip planning, without the group-chat chaos.
https://www.tripable.pro/

Team: 4CUz (Elizabeth Lu, Luke Liang, Rebecca Zhao, Thai On)
\f
CHANGE (Raskin-style)
From: planning trips in chaotic group chats + scattered links + silent friends
To: a shared trip workspace where everyone participates (availability, votes, comments, itinerary)
\f
PROBLEM
Planning with friends breaks down:
- schedules don’t align
- decisions stall (destinations / activities)
- links get buried
- 1 person becomes the “trip planner”
\f
WHY NOW
- More group travel among students/young professionals (weekends, break trips)
- Messaging apps are still the default “planning tool” (but not built for decisions)
- Collaborative UI patterns are now standard (votes, comments, shared boards)
\f
SOLUTION
Tripable = collaborative trip workspace
Tabs (blank canvases you can fill together):
1) Availability  2) Places & Activities  3) Budget  4) Itinerary
Built-in: voting + comments + shared ownership
\f
PRODUCT (AHA MOMENT)
Everyone marks availability → Tripable highlights best dates
Then the group votes on places/activities and turns winners into a realistic itinerary
\f
COMPETITION / STATUS QUO
Status quo: iMessage/WhatsApp + Google Docs + Doodle + Splitwise + “someone summarizes”
Point solutions: TripIt/Wanderlog (itinerary), Splitwise (expenses), polls (voting)
Tripable integrates the full group workflow in one place.
\f
TRACTION (EARLY)
- MVP live: tripable.pro
- User discovery: ~10 interviews (Trip planning pain patterns)
- Early growth plan: Reddit + Discord + campus groups
\f
BUSINESS MODEL
Freemium:
- Free: trip workspace + voting + comments
- Paid: itinerary optimization, budget automation, premium templates
Future: affiliate/booking revenue (lodging, activities)
\f
MARKET SIZE (BOTTOM-UP, DRAFT)
Start: students/young professionals planning 2–4 group trips/year
Initial wedge: NYC-area university communities → expand to broader 18–30 group travel
