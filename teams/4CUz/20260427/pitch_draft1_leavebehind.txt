TRIPABLE — Leave-behind deck (Draft 1)
Group trip planning, without the group-chat chaos.
https://www.tripable.pro/

Team: 4CUz (Elizabeth Lu, Luke Liang, Rebecca Zhao, Thai On)
\f
OPENING / CHANGE
From chaotic group chats (buried links, silent voters, unclear decisions)
To a shared trip workspace where every friend can contribute asynchronously:
availability + places/activities + budgets + itinerary, with voting + comments built in
\f
PROBLEM (1/2): Coordination
Group trip planning fails because:
- Availability is hard to aggregate (people respond at different times)
- Decisions require consensus (destinations, activities, lodging)
- Context gets lost when planning happens in chat
\f
PROBLEM (2/2): Unequal effort
In most groups:
- One person becomes the organizer
- Others “react” instead of helping
Result: stress, slow planning, and sometimes no trip at all
\f
WHO HAS THIS PROBLEM
Primary: college + grad students + young professionals (18–30)
Typical trip types:
- weekend/day trips
- break travel (spring/winter)
- post-semester trips
\f
CURRENT WORKAROUNDS (STATUS QUO)
People stitch together tools:
- group chat for coordination
- Google Doc/Sheet for notes + links
- Doodle/When2Meet for dates
- Airbnb/flight links in chat
- Splitwise/spreadsheet for expenses
Pain: constant context switching + manual summarization
\f
WHY NOW
- Group travel is frequent for 18–30 (short trips are common)
- Collaboration norms are established (shared docs/boards, comments, votes)
- Yet trip planning still defaults to unstructured chat threads
Opportunity: a purpose-built collaborative workspace for the full planning workflow
\f
SOLUTION (WHAT IT IS)
Tripable is a “trip workspace” your group shares:
- Tabs act like blank pages for brainstorming
- Everyone can add options, comment, and vote
- The workspace turns decisions into a plan (dates → picks → itinerary)
\f
WORKSPACE MODEL (CORE IDEA)
Each trip has tabs that behave like canvases:
1) Availability: mark conflicts; see group overlap
2) Places & Activities: add options; attach links; comment; vote
3) Budget: propose ranges; track shared costs; split plan
4) Itinerary: convert winning items into a timeline
Design goal: collaborative + less stressful, not “one person managing”
\f
PRODUCT (CORE FLOW)
1) Create trip, invite friends
2) Everyone marks availability
3) Add destinations/activities; vote + discuss
4) Choose winners; generate a realistic itinerary
5) Track/split shared expenses
Outcome: clear plan that the whole group helped create
\f
PRODUCT (AHA MOMENT)
The “aha” is participation:
Availability view reduces back-and-forth
Voting/comments turns a messy debate into visible decisions
Itinerary tab turns “winning ideas” into a plan you can execute
\f
COMPETITION (POINT SOLUTIONS)
Point tools solve slices of the workflow:
- Itinerary tools (e.g., itinerary builders)
- Expense tools (Splitwise)
- Polls (simple voting)
- Scheduling (When2Meet/Doodle)
Tripable’s bet: the value is in integrating the full group workflow into one shared space.
\f
COMPETITION (STATUS QUO IS THE BIGGEST)
The real competitor is “doing nothing new”:
iMessage/WhatsApp + Google Docs + links + reminders
Tripable wins by:
- making decisions legible (votes/comments)
- making participation easy (async contribution)
- reducing organizer burden (shared ownership)
\f

- 50%+ of invited members contribute (vote/comment/add)
- 1 completed itinerary per group
