## Overview

You've done user testing and watched people use your product. Now we need to measure what you observed at scale. This assignment asks you to prove that your analytics are live, show your conversion funnel with real numbers, and write a one-page analysis of what you're seeing.

This is not a dashboard showcase. The goal is to demonstrate that you can look at data and draw conclusions that change what you build.

## Three Components

**1. Screenshots of your dashboards**

Include screenshots from both:
- GA4 Acquisition Overview
- Amplitude Event Segmentation

These prove your analytics are installed and collecting real data.

**2. Your funnel with actual numbers**

Define your critical path (3-5 steps). Show the conversion rate at each step. Include the numbers even if they're small.

**3. Written analysis (1 page max)**

Answer three questions:
- Where is your biggest drop-off?
- What do you think is causing it?
- What will you do about it?

---

## What a Good Analysis Looks Like

Here is the format to follow:

"We drove 45 visitors from Reddit and 12 from Instagram DMs. Of the 45 Reddit visitors, 18 signed up (40%). Of the 12 Instagram visitors, 2 signed up (17%). Reddit is clearly our stronger channel.

Of the 20 total signups, 8 completed onboarding (40%). The biggest drop-off is between account creation and completing their first [action] — 12 people created accounts but never finished setup. Based on our user testing, the setup form is too long. We're cutting it from 5 fields to 2 this week."

Numbers + insight + action. That's the formula.

---

## Even If Your Numbers Are Small

5 users is enough to learn from.

- 5 signups and 0 completed onboarding? Clear signal.
- 3 of 5 users from the same channel? Clear signal.
- Nobody clicks the button you thought was obvious? Clear signal.

Don't wait for statistical significance. Look for patterns and act on them.
