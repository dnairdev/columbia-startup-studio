## Two Decks, Two Jobs

You are submitting **two separate decks**.

**Live pitch deck (10–12 slides)**
The deck you present from on Demo Day. Low text per slide — readable in 3 seconds. Reinforces your voice; does not compete with it.

**Leave-behind deck (15–20 slides)**
The deck you send after the pitch. Recognizable as similar to the live deck, but with detail that can carry itself. Full sentences, headlines that state conclusions. Stands alone without you in the room. Partners who weren't present should be able to evaluate the business from this deck alone.

---

## What Both Decks Must Cover

Focus on weaving a strong narrative above all. Strict adherence to a structure is less valuable than a compelling narrative. 

1. Title / cover
2. Opening slide — Raskin-style change statement
3. Problem
4. Why Now
5. Solution
6. Product (GIF, video, or live walkthrough of your biggest aha moment)
7. Competition (including status quo)
8. Traction (show the slope)
9. Business Model
10. Market Size (bottom-up)
11. Team (move it earlier if it's load-bearing)
12. The Ask

The leave-behind can go deeper on each section — add supporting data, appendix slides, and context that would slow down a live pitch but matters to a reader.
