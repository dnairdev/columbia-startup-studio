## Overview

You've spent the semester building a product, talking to users, and collecting data. Now you need to tell that story to a room full of people in 8 minutes.

This is your first draft — not a polished final version. The goal is to get something on paper so we can give you real feedback before Demo Day. A rough draft with a tight argument is more valuable than a beautiful deck with nothing to say.

## What Your Deck Must Include

10–12 slides covering:

1. Cover / Title
2. Problem
3. Why Now
4. Solution
5. Product (GIF, video, or live walkthrough of your biggest aha moment)
6. Market Size (bottom-up)
7. Traction (show the slope)
8. Business Model
9. Competition (including status quo)
10. Team (move it earlier if it's load-bearing)
11. The Ask

This is the live pitch deck. The leave-behind version can come later.

---

## What Makes a Good Draft

**Narrative first.** Before you open a slide tool, write out your story in a Google Doc. Answer: what changed in the world, what that means for your user, what your product does about it, and why you.

**Show the slope, not just the point.** $10K MRR growing 30% week-over-week is more compelling than $50K MRR flat. If your traction is small, show the trajectory.

**Be specific on The Ask.** "We're raising $X at $Y valuation to hit Z milestone by [date]" is a real ask. "We're looking for support" is not.

---

## What to Cut

- Your founding story (unless it's unusually load-bearing)
- The full product tour
- Architecture diagrams
- Roadmap beyond 6 months
- Hockey-stick projections

An 8-minute pitch is ~1,200 words. Treat it like an essay where every paragraph has to do real work.
