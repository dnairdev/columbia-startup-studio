## INTRO (read this verbatim)

> "Thanks for helping us out today. I'm going to have you try to use our product. A few things before we start:
>
> Please think out loud as you go — say whatever's on your mind. Whatever you're looking at, whatever you're wondering, say it.
>
> I won't help you or answer questions during the test. This might feel awkward, but it's important — we want to see how the product performs, not how well I can explain it.
>
> There are no wrong answers. We're testing the product, not you. If something is confusing, that's valuable information for us.
> 
> We'll be recording the audio and screen portion of this so that we can review again later. That was noted in the informed consent document that you signed, but I just want to mention it again. 
>
> Any questions before we start?"

## TASKS (customize this)

### Task 1 — First impression
**Say:** "Go ahead and open the link I've sent you. Take a look at the page and tell me what you think this product does."

*Let them look for 30–60 seconds. Don't explain anything.*

What to watch for:
- Do they understand the value proposition from the headline alone?
- What words do they use to describe it? (These are your users' words, not yours.)
- What confuses them?

---

### Task 2 — Sign up
**Say:** "Go ahead and try to sign up."

*Don't help. Let them struggle.*

What to watch for:
- Where do they hesitate or pause?
- Do they look for something that isn't there?
- Do they complete the flow or give up?
- How long does it take?

---

### Task 3 — Your core action *(customize this)*
**Say:** *(Write your product-specific prompt here. It should lead directly to your aha moment.)*

Examples:
- "Now try to find something you'd want to do this weekend."
- "Go ahead and create your first [thing]."
- "Try to [action that demonstrates core value]."

What to watch for:
- Do they reach the aha moment?
- What's the first moment they seem to "get it"?
- What obstacles appear between sign-up and that moment?

---

## POST-SESSION QUESTIONS

After tasks are complete, have a brief conversation. These are open-ended — let them talk.

1. "What was the first moment you felt like you understood what this is for?"

2. "What was the most confusing part of the experience?"

3. "What would have made it easier?"

4. "Is there anything you expected to find that wasn't there?"

5. "Would you use this again? Why or why not?"

6. "If you could wave a magic wand and change any one thing about the product, what would it be?"
